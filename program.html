Pixels to Patients (P2P-CV)

Bridging Computer Vision State-of-the-Art with Clinical Impact — WACV 2026

About the Workshop

Pixels to Patients (P2P-CV) is a full-day workshop at WACV 2026 dedicated to bridging the gap between cutting-edge computer vision research and its safe, effective deployment in real-world clinical practice.

Medical imaging remains one of the most socially impactful applications of CV, yet significant challenges persist in translating algorithms from laboratory prototypes into trustworthy clinical tools. This workshop provides a platform for dialogue among researchers, clinicians, and industry experts to explore the full translation pipeline — from data and model development to validation, monitoring, and regulation.

  • Explore how foundation models and multimodal learning can advance healthcare AI.
  • Discuss real-world deployment, fairness, and interoperability challenges.
  • Foster collaborations across academic, clinical, and industrial communities.

Call for Papers (CFP)

We invite original research contributions that present technical innovations while addressing the translation of computer vision into clinical practice.

Relevant Topics

  • Foundation models for medical imaging: pre-training, fine-tuning, and adaptation
  • Vision–language and multimodal learning for clinical decision support
  • Domain adaptation and generalization across sites and populations
  • Clinical deployment: validation, continual learning, and monitoring
  • Trustworthy and explainable AI for safety and interpretability
  • Fairness and equity in medical AI
  • Data-efficient learning: active, weak, and self-supervised methods
  • Quantitative imaging biomarkers and prognosis modeling
  • Human–AI collaboration and interfaces for clinician-AI teamwork

Tracks

  1. Regular Papers (≤ 8 pages, excluding references): archival papers presenting complete, original research. Published in the official WACV 2026 Workshop Proceedings.
  2. Extended Abstracts (2–4 pages, excluding references): non-archival submissions for preliminary, ongoing, or negative-result studies. Ideal for early-stage work and discussion.
  3. Problem-Pitch Proposals (≤ 2 slides): non-archival, designed for clinicians or industry partners to present concrete unmet clinical challenges and invite collaboration.

All submissions undergo rigorous review by the program committee under a double-blind policy. Regular Papers and Extended Abstracts will be peer-reviewed; Problem Pitches will be curated by the organizers for clarity and collaborative potential.

Submission Instructions

Manuscripts must follow the WACV 2026 formatting guidelines and be fully anonymized. All accepted Regular Papers will appear in the official WACV 2026 workshop proceedings.

  • Submit via OpenReview.
  • Include clear statements of clinical relevance, deployment challenges, and data limitations.
  • At least one author must register and present at the workshop.

Motivation & Impact

Despite remarkable progress in computer vision, a gap remains between research breakthroughs and real-world clinical deployment. This workshop examines healthcare as a case study for robust and trustworthy CV deployment.

  • Identify methodological, infrastructural, and regulatory barriers to safe deployment.
  • Encourage collaboration among CV researchers, clinicians, and industry leaders.
  • Promote fairness, transparency, and reproducibility in clinical AI pipelines.

By grounding technical challenges in the high-stakes context of patient care, P2P-CV aims to ensure that state-of-the-art vision systems evolve into trustworthy, clinically meaningful, and equitable AI tools.
